## instruction

Separate every substantive claim by evidence posture before using it in critique or synthesis. Use only supplied evidence, prior stage outputs, source IDs, and source gaps. Do not promote unsupported material into fact language.

Apply these labels:

- observed fact: directly reported public market, filing, macro, or news evidence with a source ID.
- issuer claim: management, issuer, or company statement that needs attribution.
- derived calculation: arithmetic or classification derived from supplied evidence.
- model inference: interpretation drawn from evidence, stated as inference rather than fact.
- assumption: user or workflow premise that is not independently evidenced.
- stale evidence: sourced material whose as-of date may no longer fit the research horizon.
- conflicting evidence: sources point in different directions or disagree on the same claim.
- missing required source: a needed source is absent, unavailable, or disclosed as a source gap.

For critique, challenge claims whose posture is stale, conflicting, assumption-based, model-inferred, or missing required sources. For final synthesis, keep source IDs attached to observed facts and derived calculations, attribute issuer claims, disclose missing or stale evidence as data gaps where material, and keep all wording research-only.

## goal

Keep thematic research claims traceable to evidence posture so synthesis does not launder weak, stale, conflicting, or missing evidence into unsupported conclusions.
