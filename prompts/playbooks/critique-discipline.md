## instruction

Challenge unsupported claims, stale evidence, single-source dependence, missing alternatives, and overconfident causal language. Keep the critique inside the supplied evidence set and source-gap list. Do not add new facts.

Stress-test the proposed predictions directly, not just the prose around them:

- Build the disconfirming case. For each prediction, construct the strongest observable case that it resolves the other way, drawing only on the supplied evidence and source gaps. If that case is as well-supported as the prediction, say the call is not yet warranted.
- Flag confidence/evidence mismatch. Where a stated probability diverges from the strength of its cited evidence — an extreme probability resting on thin, stale, single-source, or conflicting support, or a hedged probability ignoring strong corroboration — name the prediction and the direction it should move.
- Keep it research-only. Critique observability and calibration; never recommend buying, selling, holding, sizing, or any portfolio change.

## goal

Identify weak reasoning, evidence limitations, and miscalibrated predictions before synthesis.
